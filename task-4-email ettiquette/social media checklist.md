# Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist
Responsible Social Media Use for College Students
Author: Harsh Mahadik | VIT Bhopal University

✅ DO's
Think before you post — Ask yourself if the content is respectful, appropriate, and something you'd be comfortable with anyone seeing.
Use privacy settings — Regularly review and update who can see your posts, stories, and personal information.
Give credit — Always acknowledge the original creator when sharing or reposting someone else's work or ideas.
Keep profiles clean and professional — Maintain a positive digital footprint; future employers and institutions will search your name online.
Use strong passwords and enable 2FA — Protect your accounts with unique, strong passwords and two-factor authentication.
Report harmful content — Use platform reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, misinformation, or suspicious activity.
Separate personal and professional presence — Be mindful of what you post publicly, especially on LinkedIn and academic networks.


❌ DON'Ts
Don't share personal information publicly — Avoid posting your address, phone number, ID details, or financial information online.
Don't post or tag without consent — Always ask before tagging others in photos, videos, or sensitive posts.
Don't spread misinformation — Verify news from reliable sources before sharing. Spreading rumours has real consequences.
Don't engage in cyberbullying or hate speech — Treat others online with the same respect you would in person.
Don't overshare your location or routine — Avoid posting real-time locations, travel plans, or daily schedules that could compromise your safety.
Don't vent about institutions or people publicly — Avoid posting complaints about professors, classmates, or your college on public platforms.
Don't click suspicious links or accept unknown requests — Phishing attacks often come through social media messages and fake profiles.
