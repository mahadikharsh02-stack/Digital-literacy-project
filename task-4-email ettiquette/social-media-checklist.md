# Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist
### Responsible Social Media Use for College Students
**Author:** Harsh Mahadik | VIT Bhopal University

## ✅ DO's

1. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself if the content is respectful, appropriate, and something you'd be comfortable with anyone seeing.
2. **Use privacy settings** — Regularly review and update who can see your posts, stories, and personal information.
3. **Give credit** — Always acknowledge the original creator when sharing or reposting someone else's work or ideas.
4. **Keep profiles clean and professional** — Maintain a positive digital footprint; future employers and institutions will search your name online.
5. **Use strong passwords and enable 2FA** — Protect your accounts with unique, strong passwords and two-factor authentication.
6. **Report harmful content** — Use platform reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, misinformation, or suspicious activity.
7. **Separate personal and professional presence** — Be mindful of what you post publicly, especially on LinkedIn and academic networks.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** — Avoid posting your address, phone number, ID details, or financial information online.
2. **Don't post or tag without consent** — Always ask before tagging others in photos, videos, or sensitive posts.
3. **Don't spread misinformation** — Verify news from reliable sources before sharing. Spreading rumours has real consequences.
4. **Don't engage in cyberbullying or hate speech** — Treat others online with the same respect you would in person.
5. **Don't overshare your location or routine** — Avoid posting real-time locations, travel plans, or daily schedules that could compromise your safety.
6. **Don't vent about institutions or people publicly** — Avoid posting complaints about professors, classmates, or your college on public platforms.
7. **Don't click suspicious links or accept unknown requests** — Phishing attacks often come through social media messages and fake profiles.
