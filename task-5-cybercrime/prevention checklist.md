# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
**Author:** Harsh Mahadik | VIT Bhopal University  
**Task:** Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness | Digital Ambassador Programme

---

## 🛡️ General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across platforms.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts including email, social media, and banking apps. This adds a critical second layer of security.

- [ ] **3. Keep your apps and devices updated** — software updates often include security patches that protect against newly discovered vulnerabilities.

- [ ] **4. Avoid using public Wi-Fi** for sensitive tasks like banking or logging into personal accounts. If necessary, use a trusted VPN.

- [ ] **5. Think before you click** — never open links or attachments from unknown senders, even if the message looks urgent or official.

- [ ] **6. Check app permissions regularly** — revoke access to apps that do not need your camera, microphone, contacts, or location data.

---

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory)

- [ ] **7. Remember: receiving money NEVER requires your UPI PIN** — if anyone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive" a payment, it is a scam. Disconnect immediately.

- [ ] **8. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV** with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, Google Pay, or PhonePe support.

- [ ] **9. Always verify the UPI ID or QR code** before making a payment — double-check the recipient's name that appears after scanning or entering the ID.

- [ ] **10. Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app to minimise losses in the event of unauthorised access.

---

## 📱 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **11. Do not post personal details publicly** — avoid sharing your Aadhaar number, PAN card, phone number, or home address on any platform.

- [ ] **12. Verify before you trust** — if you receive a message from a "friend" or "family member" asking for urgent money, call them directly to confirm before sending anything.

---

## 🚨 Reporting Channels

If you encounter or fall victim to any cybercrime, report it immediately:

| Channel | Details |
|--------|---------|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| 📞 **National Cyber Helpline** | **1930** (available 24/7) |
| 🏦 **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Call the number on the back of your debit/credit card |
| 🚔 **Local Cyber Police Station** | File an FIR for financial fraud above ₹1 lakh |

> ⚡ **Act fast** — report within the first hour of fraud for the highest chance of fund recovery.
