# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud
**Cybercrime Type:** UPI/Online Payment Fraud  
**Author:** Harsh Mahadik | VIT Bhopal University  
**Reference:** Based on real incident patterns reported by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) and RBI Fraud Reports (2022–2023)

---

## What is UPI/Online Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of financial cybercrime in which attackers manipulate victims into authorising fraudulent transactions — or steal their UPI credentials — to illegally transfer money from their bank accounts. India, being one of the largest UPI markets in the world, sees thousands of such cases every month.

---

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Target Selection:** The attacker identifies a victim — often someone who has recently posted on OLX, Quikr, or social media about selling an item or seeking financial help.
2. **Initial Contact:** The fraudster contacts the victim posing as a genuine buyer, army officer, or bank official, expressing urgency and trust.
3. **The Deception:** The attacker sends a *collect request* on the victim's UPI app, falsely claiming it will "send" money to them. The victim, confused by the interface, unknowingly approves the payment.
4. **PIN Entry:** The victim enters their UPI PIN, believing they are receiving funds — but the PIN authorises an outgoing transfer.
5. **Money Transferred:** Funds are instantly debited and moved to a mule account, often withdrawn within minutes.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-time UPI users and senior citizens unfamiliar with the interface
- Students and individuals selling items on second-hand platforms
- Small business owners and daily wage earners
- People seeking loans or financial assistance online

---

## Consequences

- **Financial:** Victims lose anywhere from ₹500 to several lakhs instantly; recovery is extremely difficult.
- **Psychological:** Victims experience stress, shame, and loss of trust in digital payment systems.
- **Legal:** Reporting is complex; many cases go unresolved due to quick fund movement across mule accounts.
- **Social:** Fraudsters exploit emotional trust, leaving lasting trauma beyond just monetary loss.

---

## What Could Be Done Differently?

- Always remember: **receiving money never requires your UPI PIN.**
- Verify the identity of anyone requesting a transaction before approving.
- Report suspicious requests immediately at **cybercrime.gov.in** or call **1930** (National Cyber Helpline).
